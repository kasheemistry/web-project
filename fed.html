THE STUDENT SAVER


How to stay fed

From our experince cooking can be very difficult for students; knowing what to cook, how to cook it, and the long time it takes are just a few of the reasons that put us off from cooking.

Eating 3 healthy (and proper) meals a day is essential to getting enough energy to cope with the demands of being a student, so here's some handy information in making cooking a lot easier and more manageable.




What to buy?

First Image

Baked beans is the perfect comfort food, super easy to cook in the microwave and can be eaten with pretty much anything.

Second Image

Next up: Protein. A lot of students cut meat out, either because its too expensive or they just don't know what to do with it. We'd recommend buying multipacks of chicken breast because it is cheaper, and breast meat is lean and requires less cooking time (ideal for busy students!) If you are vegetarian, ignore what we have just said, and some great alternatives are chickpeas, lentils or eggs.

Third Image

Well, pasta is a uni stdents' best friend: it's versatile, easy to make and very cheap! It also requires little cooking skills.

Hints and Tips


  1. Microwaves are your new best friend. Did you know you can cook an egg in the microwave? Or boil some vegetables? A quick and easy wasy to make a wholesome meal!

  2. Cook in larger batches so you can freeze the leftover food in labelled containers, meaning you don't have to cook as often!

  3. Create meals with leftovers, it's so easy to waste food and make new meals from scratch when you can easily add lefover chicken into pasta, fajitas, or sandwiches.
